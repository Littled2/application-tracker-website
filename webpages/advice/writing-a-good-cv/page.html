How to Write a CV: A Step-by-Step Guide

Your CV is often your first impression on potential employers. Here's how to make it count.

December 23 2024, by Ed Blewitt

What Is a CV?

A CV, or Curriculum Vitae, is a document that outlines your educational background, professional experience, skills, and achievements. It should be tailored to the job you are applying for and serves as a summary of your qualifications.

Step-by-Step Guide to Writing a CV

1. Start with Your Contact Information

Include your name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile (if applicable). Ensure this section is clearly visible at the top of your CV.

2. Write a Strong Personal Statement

Provide a brief summary of your career goals, skills, and what you bring to the role. Keep it concise and tailored to the job.

3. Outline Your Work Experience

  • List your professional experience in reverse chronological order.
  • Include job titles, company names, locations, and employment dates.
  • Highlight your responsibilities and achievements using bullet points.

4. Highlight Your Education

Include your degrees, institutions, and graduation dates. Mention relevant coursework or honors if applicable.

5. Showcase Your Skills

List technical and soft skills relevant to the job. Be specific, such as "Proficient in Python" or "Strong communication and leadership abilities."

6. Add Additional Sections

  • Certifications: Include any professional certifications relevant to the role.
  • Languages: Mention any additional languages you speak and your proficiency level.
  • Volunteer Work: Highlight volunteer experiences that demonstrate your skills or commitment.

7. Keep It Clean and Organized

Use consistent formatting, such as the same font and spacing throughout. Avoid clutter and stick to a one- or two-page length.

Top Tips for a Winning CV

  • Tailor Your CV: Customize it for each job application to emphasize relevant skills and experience.
  • Proofread: Check for grammar and spelling errors.
  • Use Action Words: Start bullet points with verbs like "achieved," "developed," or "led."
  • Quantify Achievements: Use numbers to show impact, such as "increased sales by 20%."

Conclusion

Writing a CV may seem daunting, but with careful planning and attention to detail, you can create a document that stands out to employers. Tailor your CV to each role, focus on achievements, and always keep it professional.
